Tourist Attractions

Unveil the Marvels of Bacolod City: From the captivating splendor of The Ruins to the enchanting vistas of Campuestohan Highland Resort, immerse yourself in the allure of Balay Negrense, the tranquility of San Sebastian Cathedral, the cultural richness of Negros Museum, and the serenity of Capitol Park and Lagoon. Experience the essence of Bacolod through its iconic landmarks!

The Ruins

The Lacson Ruins is the ancestral mansion of the family of wealthy sugar businessman Don Mariano Ledesma Lacson built on a 440 hectare sugar plantation in Talisay, Negros Occidental, in the early 1900s in memory of his Portuguese wife Maria Braga Lacson, who died during the birth of their 8th child. During the Second World War, Filipino guerillas burned it down as a countermeasure to prevent the invading Japanese forces from using it as a military headquarters. It burned for three days. The intention was to burn it to the ground.

Campuestohan Highland Resort

Campuestohan Highland Resort is a fun-filled tourist destination located between the cities of Bacolod and Talisay in Negros Occidental. The five-hectare highland resort is one of the go-to places not only for Negrenses but also travelers from different parts of the country because of its cool temperatures. The view of the Negros Occidental province and even Panay Island will leave nature lovers amazed.

Balay Negrense

The Balay Negrense, also known as the Victor Fernandez Gaston Ancestral House, is a museum located in Negros Occidental, Philippines. Built in 1897, it showcases the lifestyle of a late 19th-century Negrense sugar baron, Victor F. Gaston, who was a pioneer in sugarcane cultivation in the region. Originally Gaston's ancestral home, it housed him and his twelve children until his death in 1927. Abandoned in the mid-1970s, the house was later restored through efforts by Msgr. Guillermo Ma. Gaston and the Negros Cultural Foundation. Donated to the Philippine Tourism Authority in 1992, it was repaired and furnished with period furniture, officially opening as a museum in 1990. Recognized as a heritage house by the National Historical Commission of the Philippines, it stands as a testament to the rich history and cultural heritage of Negros Occidental.

Negros Museum

The Negros Museum, located within the Negros Occidental Provincial Capitol Complex in Bacolod, Philippines, was originally constructed in 1925 as the Provincial Agriculture Building. Opened on March 17, 1996, the museum was relocated to this building after its former location in the Provincial Capitol was reclaimed for government use in 2003. Unlike traditional museums, the Negros Museum focuses on showcasing the lifestyle and society of Negrense culture, featuring old furnishings and loaned items from ancestral houses. It also hosts art exhibitions from local and foreign artists residing in the Negros Island Region, along with offering art training and seminars.

Capitol Park and Lagoon

In the Philippine province of Negros Occidental, Bacolod is home to the Capitol Park and Lagoon. Matching sculptures of a man pulling a second water buffalo and a woman standing next to one are among the park's attractions. The northern and southern extremities of the lagoon are where you can see these statues. Italian sculptor Francesco Riccardo Monti created these figures. He is also the artist behind the sculptures at the University of the Philippines - Visayas Iloilo campus and the Metropolitan Manila Theater. In addition, Guillermo Tolentino made a contribution by sculpting the statues.
