Origins of MassKara Festival

The MassKara Festival, held annually every 4th Sunday of October in Bacolod, Philippines, is a vibrant celebration featuring highlights at various sites including the Bacolod Public Plaza, the Lacson Tourism Strip, and the Bacolod City Government Center. The term "MassKara" is a portmanteau created by the late artist Ely Santiago, combining "mass" (representing a multitude of people) with the Spanish word "cara" (meaning face), resulting in "MassKara" (signifying a multitude of faces). Additionally, it's a play on the Filipino word "maskara," meaning "mask" in Spanish, which is a central element of the festival, typically adorned with smiling faces. This association has led to Bacolod being affectionately known as the "City of Smiles." The MassKara Festival originated in 1980 during a time of tragedy when the MV Don Juan sank, resulting in loss of lives. To uplift spirits, Bacolod's local government initiated the festival as a celebration of resilience and unity, coinciding with the city's Charter Day. Over the years, the festival has grown into a major tourism attraction, showcasing the city's culture and creativity. The addition of the Electric MassKara further enhanced the festival's appeal, featuring illuminated floats and vibrant performances. Despite its somber beginnings, the festival has become a symbol of Bacolod's resilience and vitality, with the 2019 celebration marking its 40th anniversary, aptly named Ruby MassKara.

Main Features of the Festival

The MassKara Festival showcases a vibrant street dance competition where masked dancers move to Latin beats, demonstrating skill, joy, and endurance. Alongside, festivities include the MassKara Queen pageant, carnivals, musical concerts, food festivals, and cultural exhibits. The Electric MassKara parade dazzles with music, lights, and floats, while street dancing, divided into school and barangay divisions, takes center stage, reflecting Bacolod's lively spirit and rich heritage.

Significance and Impact

The MassKara Festival holds profound significance for Bacolod City, serving as a vibrant celebration of its resilience, creativity, and unity. This annual event has become synonymous with Bacolod's cultural identity, showcasing its artistic talent and festive spirit through colorful street dancing and lively performances. Beyond its cultural importance, MassKara is a major driver of tourism and economic growth, attracting visitors from across the Philippines and beyond, thereby stimulating the local economy and supporting businesses in hospitality, entertainment, and retail sectors. More than just a festival, MassKara fosters a sense of community pride and solidarity among Bacolodnons, bringing together people from diverse backgrounds to celebrate their city's culture and achievements. By promoting arts, culture, and social cohesion, MassKara continues to play a vital role in shaping Bacolod's identity and enriching the lives of its residents.
